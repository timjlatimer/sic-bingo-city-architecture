## The Swiss Federal System

Switzerland is governed under a federal system at three levels: the Confederation (federal government), 26 cantons, and approximately 2,100 communes. The foundational principle is **subsidiarity** (Article 5a, Federal Constitution): nothing that can be done at a lower political level should be done at a higher level. Powers are delegated upward only when necessary.

## The Gemeindeversammlung (Commune Assembly) = The Rooftop Meeting

Over 1,650 Swiss municipalities use citizen assemblies instead of elected local parliaments. Citizens gather in community halls, discuss issues, and make decisions by raising hands. Key features that map to the Bingo City rooftop:

| Swiss Feature | Bingo City Equivalent |

| Citizens meet as equals | Swarm Mind Chorus mode — equal voice |

| Decisions broadly accepted despite low participation | Governance legitimacy through quality of debate, not quantity |
| Twice-yearly gatherings with agenda and budget | Daily stand-ups + periodic governance reviews |

## The Federal Council = Collegial Leadership

The Swiss Federal Council has 7 members who govern as equals, making decisions by consensus. The presidency rotates annually and is largely ceremonial. There is no prime minister with executive authority.

**Bingo City modification:** The Pope (or Master Jeeves at org level) retains a **benevolent dictator override** — but it must be transparent, justified, rare, and reviewable. This is constitutional reserve power, not autocracy.
