## Executive Summary

Marketing in the HB1000 ecosystem is not a single function — it is a **multi-level, multi-directional system** that operates simultaneously at the brand level (above the line), the initiative level (inside each building), and the community level (within Ruby Red's world). The core recommendation of this research is:

> **Marketing should NOT have its own building in Bingo City. Instead, it should be embedded as a dedicated floor (F3 — Intelligence/Amplification) in every existing building, with a Marketing Grace sub-agent on each building's rooftop inner ring, all coordinated by a single "Brand Godmother" agent that reports to the Grace Godmother.**

This architecture reflects the reality that marketing is a cross-cutting capability, not a standalone initiative. It also reflects the HB1000's philosophy that the best marketing is not a department — it is the natural byproduct of doing excellent work and telling the story of that work with authenticity.

## Part 1: The Framework — Two Levels of Marketing

Before answering where Marketing lives, we must distinguish between the two fundamentally different types of marketing in the HB1000 ecosystem:

### Level 1: Above-the-Line Marketing (The Moonshot Brand)

This is marketing the SIC brand, the HB1000 philosophy, and the moonshot vision. Its audience is NOT Ruby Red Maven. Its audience is:

- Philanthropists and impact investors who fund the ecosystem
- Policy makers and civic leaders who can change the systems Ruby Red navigates
- Corporate partners who can adopt the LDP and Blue Seal
- Media and thought leaders who can amplify the SIC message
- Other organizations who want to join the Honeybee Zone

**The North Star for above-the-line marketing:** "Touch the hand of someone who changed the world." This is Tim's personal North Star. Above-the-line marketing is the mechanism by which the SIC brand becomes known to the people who can help change the world.

**Where it lives:** Above the HB1000 line. It is NOT inside Bingo City. It is the strategic communications layer that sits above all six buildings. It is the responsibility of the CVO (Tim) and the Brand Godmother agent.

### Level 2: Below-the-Line Marketing (Initiative-Specific Amplification)

This is marketing each individual initiative to its specific audience. Each initiative has a different audience and a different marketing challenge:

| Initiative | Primary Audience | Marketing Challenge |
|------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Cloud Butterfly | Tim and the Solve Team | Internal — not a public product yet |
| Wisdom Giants | Global knowledge seekers | Awareness — nobody knows it exists yet |
| Digger Cafe | Local community members | Foot traffic and community activation |
| Effn Duck | Advocacy community, media | Credibility and reach |
| PTK Promises | Organizations and entrepreneurs | B2B trust and adoption |
| Seba Hub | Ruby Red Maven | Trust, safety, and relevance |

**Where it lives:** Inside each building, as a dedicated floor. The specific floor depends on the initiative's current stage.

## Part 2: Marketing Inside Bingo City — The Floor Placement Question

### Option A: Marketing as F3 (Intelligence/Amplification) in Every Building

**Recommendation: ADOPT THIS.**

In the F1-F5 framework, F3 is the "Intelligence" layer — where the initiative learns from what it's doing and amplifies its impact. This is the natural home for marketing within each building.

Renaming F3 from "Intelligence" to "Intelligence & Amplification" for all buildings would make the marketing function explicit without creating a separate floor.

**What F3 — Intelligence & Amplification contains:**
- Community data and feedback loops
- Story capture (the Jolene Protocol applied to initiative stories)
- Content creation sub-agent (Grace-powered)
- Social amplification sub-agent
- JOY nudges and community activation
- Progress reporting to the above-the-line Brand Godmother

**Why F3 and not a new F6:**
Adding an F6 "Marketing" floor to every building would create artificial complexity and suggest that marketing is something you do AFTER the work is done. In reality, marketing is most effective when it is embedded in the work from the beginning. F3 is the right layer because it is where the initiative's intelligence and community integration live — and great marketing IS intelligence about your audience combined with authentic community connection.

### Option B: Marketing as a New Building (F7 — The Broadcast Tower)

**Recommendation: REJECT for now, revisit at scale.**

A 7th building in Bingo City dedicated to Marketing would make sense if the SIC brand reaches a scale where marketing becomes a full-time, multi-person operation with its own bingo squares and progress tracking. At current scale, this would be premature.

However, the concept of a "Broadcast Tower" building — a tall, antenna-topped structure that amplifies signals from all other buildings — is a compelling future addition. When the SIC brand is ready for a dedicated marketing operation, this building should be added to the city skyline.

**The Broadcast Tower concept:**
- Taller than all other buildings (represents reach and amplification)
- Windows that mirror the activity of all other buildings (it amplifies what they're doing)
- Antenna on the rooftop (visual metaphor for broadcasting)
- Color: Gold or white (represents clarity and reach)

### Option C: Marketing as Half of an "Operations" Floor

**Recommendation: REJECT.**

Combining Marketing with Operations in a single floor would subordinate marketing to operational concerns and make it invisible. Marketing is not an operational function — it is a strategic amplification function. It deserves its own identity, even if that identity is embedded within each building's F3 layer.

## Part 3: Marketing on the Rooftop Society

### The Marketing Avatar Question

Should there be a dedicated Marketing avatar on the Rooftop Society inner ring of each building?

**Recommendation: YES — but as a shared role, not a dedicated person.**

The inner ring of the Rooftop Society currently has 4 positions:
1. CVO / Tim
2. Lead Grace (AI Agent)
3. Subject Matter Expert
4. Community Voice (Ruby Red representative)

**Proposed change:** Replace Position 3 (Subject Matter Expert) with a dual role: **"Subject Matter Expert & Story Keeper."** The Story Keeper responsibility is to capture and tell the story of the initiative's progress — this is the marketing function embedded in the inner ring.

**Why not a dedicated Marketing avatar:**
Adding a 5th inner ring position specifically for Marketing would overweight the marketing function relative to the initiative's actual work. The Story Keeper role embedded in Position 3 is more honest — it says "the person closest to the work is also responsible for telling the story of the work."

### The Brand Godmother — A New Outer Ring Position

**Recommendation: ADD a "Brand Godmother" avatar to the outer ring (Position 8) of every building.**

The Brand Godmother is a Grace sub-agent that:
- Monitors the initiative's progress and extracts story-worthy moments
- Applies the Jolene Protocol to create compelling narratives
- Routes stories to the above-the-line Brand Godmother for amplification
- Tracks the initiative's "Story Health" as part of the PEARL Diamond (see Part 5)

The Brand Godmother sits in the outer ring because she is a support function, not a core team member. She shows up when there is a story to tell, not every day.

## Part 5: Marketing and the PEARL Diamond

### Should "Amplification" Replace "Alignment" in the PEARL Diamond?

**Recommendation: NO. Add "Amplification" as a sixth dimension — the PEARL-A Diamond.**

The current PEARL Diamond measures: Progress, Engagement, Alignment, Resilience, Legacy.

Marketing/Amplification does not replace any of these dimensions — it is a sixth dimension that measures how well the initiative is telling its story. The proposed PEARL-A Diamond adds:

| Letter | Dimension | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **P** | Progress | Bingo square completion rate |
| **E** | Engagement | Team activity, avatar presence |
| **A** | Alignment | North Star drift score |
| **R** | Resilience | Blocked vs. in-progress ratio |
| **L** | Legacy | Long-term impact score |
| **-A** | **Amplification** | Story health — is the initiative's story being told? |

The Amplification score measures:
- Content output frequency (is the Brand Godmother producing stories?)
- Story quality (Jolene Protocol compliance score)
- Reach (how many people are hearing the story?)
- Community response (are people sharing and engaging?)

## Part 8: The Ruby Red Marketing Principle

All below-the-line marketing in the HB1000 ecosystem must be filtered through the Ruby Red Marketing Principle:

> **"Marketing to Ruby Red is not advertising. It is invitation."**

Ruby Red has been marketed TO her entire life by the three gangsters — predatory lenders, payday loan companies, and political campaigns that exploit her fears. She has a finely tuned bullshit detector. Any marketing that feels like advertising will trigger that detector and destroy trust.

The only marketing that works for Ruby Red is:

1. **Peer testimony** — "My neighbor told me about Maven and it changed my life."
2. **Demonstrated value** — "I saw that Digger Cafe was helping people in my neighborhood."
3. **Grace's invitation** — "Grace told me about this and I trust Grace."
4. **JOY** — "This felt good, so I told my friends."

This means the most powerful marketing engine in the HB1000 is not a Content Grace or a Social Grace — it is Grace herself, and the JOY she creates in every interaction. Every time Grace makes Ruby Red feel seen, capable, and connected, Ruby Red becomes a marketing channel.

**Implication for the marketing architecture:** The Community Grace sub-agent is the most important marketing sub-agent for below-the-line work. Everything else supports it.

## Conclusion: The Three Decisions for Tim

Based on this research, three decisions are required from the CVO:

**Decision 1: Adopt the F3 "Antenna" naming convention?**
Rename F3 from "Intelligence" to "Intelligence & Amplification" (or "The Antenna") across all buildings, making the marketing function explicit within the existing floor structure.

**Decision 2: Add the Brand Godmother to the Rooftop Society outer ring?**
Add a Position 8 "Brand Godmother" Grace avatar to the outer ring of every building's Rooftop Society, creating a consistent story-capture function across all initiatives.

**Decision 3: Add Amplification to the PEARL Diamond?**
Extend the PEARL Diamond to PEARL-A by adding an "Amplification" dimension that measures story health — how well each initiative is telling its story.

These three decisions can be implemented in the current Bingo City architecture without adding a new building. The Broadcast Tower (Building 7) remains a future option for when the SIC brand reaches the scale that demands a dedicated marketing operation.
