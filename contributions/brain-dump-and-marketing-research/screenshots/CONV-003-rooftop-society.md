# CONV-003: The Rooftop Society Avatar System

**Type:** Conversation Reconstruction  
**Context:** The design of the Rooftop Society — the team avatar system on top of each building  
**Significance:** Establishes the human element in the Bingo City visualization

---

## The Core Problem Being Solved

The Initiative Dashboard without the Rooftop Society is just a data visualization. It shows progress, but it doesn't show the people. The Rooftop Society was conceived to answer the question: **"Who is working on this?"**

The rooftop metaphor was chosen because:
1. It is literally the highest point of the building — the team is "above" the work, overseeing it
2. It creates a natural "send them to work" / "call them back up" toggle — the team descends into the building to work, and returns to the rooftop when the work is done
3. It makes the building feel inhabited, not just tracked

---

## The 9-Position Layout

The Rooftop Society has 9 positions arranged in two rings plus a center:

**Inner Ring (Positions 1-4):** Core team members who are most actively engaged with the initiative. These are the people who show up every day.

**Outer Ring (Positions 5-8):** Support team members who provide periodic input, oversight, or specialized expertise. These are the people who show up when needed.

**Center Position (9):** The North Star Keeper — the person or agent responsible for ensuring the initiative never drifts from its North Star. This is a special role, not just another team member.

---

## The Toggle Button

The "Send them to work" / "Call them back up" toggle button was one of the most discussed design decisions. It serves multiple purposes:

1. **Visual feedback:** When the team is "at work," the building windows light up more actively. When they're "called back up," the building goes into a resting state.
2. **Engagement metaphor:** It makes the user feel like they are directing the team, not just observing data.
3. **Ruby Red Maven accessibility:** A simple toggle is the most accessible possible interaction. No complex menus, no technical jargon.

---

## Emoji Avatars — Why Not Photos or Illustrations

The decision to use emoji avatars rather than photos or custom illustrations was deliberate:

1. **Zero cost:** No image generation, no photography, no illustration budget
2. **Universal:** Emoji are understood worldwide
3. **Playful:** The Bingo City aesthetic is warm and approachable, not corporate
4. **Accessible:** Emoji render perfectly on all devices, including older iOS Safari versions
5. **Consistent with the no-AI-images constraint:** The entire Bingo City app uses CSS/SVG/emoji only

---

## The "Send Them to Work" Metaphor and Ruby Red

For Ruby Red Maven, the Rooftop Society metaphor is particularly resonant. She understands the concept of "people going to work" viscerally — it is her daily reality. The idea that her team is on the rooftop, ready to be sent to work on her behalf, is empowering rather than abstract.

This is consistent with the HB1000's philosophy of making abstract concepts tangible through universally understood metaphors.
