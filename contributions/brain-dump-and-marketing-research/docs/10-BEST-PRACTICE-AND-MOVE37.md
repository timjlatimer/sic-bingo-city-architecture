## The Three Best Practice Gaps

### Gap #1: Formalize Trauma-Informed Design

Our philosophy is deeply aligned with trauma-informed care, but it is not yet an explicit design principle. The constant stress of poverty rewires the brain, reducing prefrontal cortex volume and making long-term planning difficult. We must move from intuitive empathy to a formal Trauma-Informed Design Framework based on SAMHSA's six principles: Safety, Trustworthiness, Peer Support, Collaboration, Empowerment, and Cultural Sensitivity.

**Action Item:** Every feature in Maven, every word from Grace, every interaction in Big Mama's Genius Village must be filtered through these six principles.

### Gap #2: Implement a Technical AI Fairness & Bias Mitigation Framework

The LDP provides a powerful philosophical framework for integrity, but it lacks a technical counterpart. The EU AI Act and Microsoft's Responsible AI Standard demand proactive bias mitigation and explainable AI. We cannot simply trust that our intentions are good; we must prove that our algorithms are fair.

**Action Item:** Adopt a technical framework for auditing AI models for bias, particularly in any credit-scoring or opportunity-matching algorithms. This includes regular testing, transparent model cards, and a clear process for appealing algorithmic decisions.

### Gap #3: Build a Real Gamification Engine, Not Just a Points System

We have concepts for gamification (Pearl Maturity Levels, Ruby Red Impact Score), but we lack the engine. Best practices from financial wellness apps show that effective gamification is about more than just points; it's about **progress, mastery, and social connection**. Critically, for our audience, it must be designed to **avoid shame at all costs**.

**Action Item:** Design a gamification system based on personal progress and contribution to the community, not public leaderboards. Reward consistent habits (e.g., streak bonuses for saving), celebrate small wins, and create collaborative community goals.

## The Three Move 37 Opportunities

### Move 37.1: Structure Maven as a Data Cooperative

This is our most powerful, game-changing move. Instead of building a service that Ruby Red uses, we build a platform that she **owns**. By structuring Maven as a data cooperative, we give users collective ownership and governance over their data. This is the ultimate expression of data sovereignty. This single move would make us the most trusted financial platform on Earth.

**Status:** Implemented in v2.0 architecture. Maven IS a Data Cooperative.

### Move 37.2: Create a "Ruby Red Emergency Fund" with Direct Cash Transfers

Propel's most impactful action was not its app; it was raising a $10 million emergency fund and distributing direct $50 cash transfers to families during the SNAP shutdown. We must build this capability into Maven from day one.

**Status:** Implemented in v2.0 architecture. Ruby Red Emergency Fund is now a core component of Layer 10.

### Move 37.3: Build a Voice-First, Dialect-Specific AI

The WEF highlights that one of the most impactful uses of AI in financial inclusion is voice-first interfaces in local dialects. Grace should not just speak; she should speak the language of Ruby Red.

**Status:** Designed in v2.0 architecture. Pearl orchestrates dialect-specific voice model training. Not yet deployed.
