## Ruby Red — Who We Solve For

Ruby Red is the **Chief Financial Officer of the household**. She is typically a 35-45 year old mother of two who is trying to figure out how to make her finances stretch until the next payday. She is part of the working poor, left out and left behind, the unbanked and underbanked part of the community.

> **"It's expensive to be poor."** — This is the crime we're solving.

### Her Daily Decisions

- Does she put something back when she's in the grocery line?
- Does she let the kids go to a $30 extracurricular event?
- How does she pay for the flat tire on the car?

### The Science Behind Ruby Red

Poverty is a cognitive condition, not just a financial one (Mullainathan & Shafir). Scarcity reduces cognitive bandwidth by 13-14 IQ points. Every feature must RESTORE bandwidth, not consume it.

### Ruby Red's JTBD at the Emotional Level

These are survival needs, not entertainment needs:

1. Feel seen
2. Feel less alone
3. Feel capable
4. Feel connected
5. Start the day with intention

### The Three Gangsters

| Gangster | How It Hurts Ruby Red | What We Do About It |
|----------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **Political** | Learned helplessness — systems designed to keep her disengaged | Civic empowerment, information access |
| **Bureaucratic** | Cognitive punishment — forms, complexity, wait times designed for people with time and education | Simplification, AI navigation, advocacy |

### The Superpower: Empathy

> **"There but for the grace of God go I."**

This is not a passive sentiment. It is an active design principle. Every product, feature, and interaction must be infused with this empathetic understanding of Ruby Red's lived experience.

## Maven — The Community Platform

Maven is the community platform that serves Ruby Red directly. It is the implementation layer where the HB1000 philosophy meets real people. Maven's primary interface is Grace.

**As of v2.0 of the architecture, Maven is explicitly structured as a Data Cooperative.** This is not a feature — it is Maven's foundational legal and technical structure. Users collectively own and govern their data. Ruby Red's data is never sold, she benefits from collective intelligence, and she can take her data with her at any time. This single structural decision makes Maven the most trusted financial platform for the underserved.

**Big Mama's Genius Village** is the community brand within Maven — "Our Data. Our Power. Our Destiny." — fueled by JOY.

## Maven's Six Pricing Principles

Maven's pricing is not a revenue model. It is a **relationship model** that happens to involve money.

| # | Principle | What It Means |
|---|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | **Grace Never Gets Cut** | Regardless of tier — including Free — Grace is always there. Full voice, full text, full memory, full empathy. Grace is not a feature. Grace is the relationship. |
| 2 | **Nobody Gets Abandoned** | When a payment fails, Maven does not lock the door. Maven opens a smaller door. And then a smaller one. And if all fails: "You're still a member of the Village. Grace is still here." No collections. No penalty. No shame. |
| 3 | **Trust, Not Contract** | No signatures. No commitments. No fine print. Cancel anytime. Change tiers anytime. Leave and come back. |
| 4 | **Cash-Flow Friendly** | Five payment intervals (monthly, weekly, weekly lite, daily, free) so Ruby Red can match payment to her cash flow. |
| 5 | **Step-Down Is a Feature** | When Ruby Red steps down, the system is working as designed. The step-down protects her from cascading consequences (overdraft fees, credit damage). Maven absorbs the disruption. |
| 6 | **Need Milk Money Is a Hand Up** | NMM is not charity. It is a micro-lending program. Ruby Red agrees to repay. $2.50 admin fee covers costs. No interest. No penalties. No collections. |

## Need Milk Money (NMM)

Need Milk Money replaced the V1 "Community Chest" model. It is a micro-lending program controlled by Grace and Big Mama.

- Allotments: $25, $50, or $100
- Administration fee: $2.50 per allotment (covers the cost of moving the money)
- No interest charges, no penalties, no collections
- If Ruby Red cannot repay, the step-down logic absorbs it gracefully

## The Ruby Red Emergency Fund

The Ruby Red Emergency Fund is a community-governed crisis response layer that sits on top of the Need Milk Money infrastructure.

- **NMM:** Everyday instant liquidity ($25/$50/$100)
- **Emergency Fund:** Crisis-level support for larger shocks (medical bills, car breakdowns, job loss)

The fund is seeded by a portion of Maven's revenue and philanthropic partnerships. Modeled after Propel's $10M emergency fund that distributed $50 cash transfers to 230,000 families during the SNAP shutdown. When a crisis hits, the community can deploy immediate, no-strings-attached cash to those who need it most.

## Gamification in Maven

Maven's gamification is delivered through the Bingo Card and the Ticker Tape. Every card completion, savings streak, and community contribution feeds into the Gamification Engine.

**Three gamification badges:**
1. **Pearl Maturity Level** — How sophisticated the user's engagement with Pearl has become
2. **Ruby Red Impact Score** — The positive impact the user has had on their own life and community
3. **Grace Family Tree** — How many people the user has connected to the Village

**Critical design principle:** Gamification for the poor must NEVER shame. No leaderboards that expose financial status. Progress is personal, not comparative.
