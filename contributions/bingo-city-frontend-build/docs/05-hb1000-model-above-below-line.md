# HB1000 Model — Above the Line / Below the Line

**SIC HB1000 Solve Team | March 2026**

---

## The Core Distinction

The HB1000 architecture has a fundamental organizing principle: **above the line** and **below the line**. Everything in the system is either strategic (above) or tactical (below). The line is the boundary between vision and execution.

This distinction comes from the Two-Level Bingo Card Framework, which is the constitutional document for how Tim's initiatives are organized.

---

## Above the Line — The Prime Directive Layer

**What lives here:** Tim's personal North Star, the SIC brand, the mission, the movement.

**The Prime Directive:** "Touch the hand of someone who changed the world."  
This is Tim's personal life strategy. It is not an initiative. It is the reason all initiatives exist.

**The BHAG (Big Hairy Audacious Goal):** 1–2 year horizon. Jim Collins-style.

**Core Values:** The behavioral principles that govern how the team operates.

**The Prime Directive Bingo Cards:** Tim's personal life strategy expressed as a 5×5 bingo card. Each square is a major initiative or life role. This is Layer 2 of the Pearl Framework.

**Social Impact Capitalism (SIC):** The philosophy that transforms capitalism from "profit-at-any-cost" to "purpose-with-profit." The Blue Seal certification. "Business as a force for good."

**Above-the-line marketing:** Marketing the moonshot itself — the SIC brand, the mission, the movement. This is NOT marketing individual initiatives. It is marketing the idea that business can be a force for good.

## Where Bingo City Sits

Bingo City is a **below-the-line tool** that makes below-the-line work visible. It is the execution layer made visual.

However, Bingo City has an **above-the-line dimension** too: the city itself — the concept of "a city of AI advocates working for you" — is a powerful marketing message that operates above the line. The idea of Bingo City is a brand asset.

This creates an interesting architectural question: does the Bingo City concept belong above the line (as a brand/marketing asset) or below the line (as an execution tool)? The answer is: **both**. The concept is above the line. The implementation is below the line.

## The Recursive Quality (Move 37)

The most profound architectural insight is that the HB1000 model is **recursive**. The same structure that organizes Tim's initiatives can be collapsed and given to Ruby Red.

Tim has a city of initiatives. Ruby Red gets a city of advocates.  
Tim has a Rooftop Society coordinating his work. Ruby Red has a Rooftop Society working for her.  
Tim has a bingo card tracking his progress. Ruby Red has a bingo card tracking what her team is building for her.

The architecture is the same. The scale is different. The beneficiary is different. But the structure is identical.

This is Move 37 — the unexpected move that changes the game. The tool that empowers the team can be given directly to the person the team is serving.
