# Marketing Architecture Research Notes

## Key Findings from Research

### 1. Marketing Center of Excellence (CoE) Model
- A CoE is a CENTRAL HUB for strategy, best practices, and innovation
- It operates ABOVE execution teams, providing strategic oversight
- Traditional departments focus on channels/business units; CoE focuses on capability building
- CoE acts as "guardian of the brand" — establishes guidelines, provides templates
- CoE breaks down silos between marketing, sales, and product
- CoE centralizes technology procurement (economies of scale)
- CoE places customer at the heart of all strategy

### 2. Centralized vs. Decentralized Marketing
- **Centralized:** Single team, unified brand image, great for similar products/services
- **Decentralized:** Multiple teams, local autonomy, great for diverse markets/brands
- **Hybrid (Best Practice):** Central CoE + Decentralized execution teams
  - Central: Brand standards, strategy, technology, analytics
  - Local: Execution, local adaptation, community engagement

### 3. Social Enterprise Marketing Best Practices
- "Be a business first" — focus on product/service, not just impact story
- Transparency is critical (aligns with "sunlight is the best disinfectant")
- Digital marketing is essential but must be mission-aligned
- Community engagement > customer acquisition for mission-driven orgs
- Platform cooperatives emphasize stakeholder-first marketing

### 4. Shared Services Model
- Shared services = scale and efficiency for repeatable, transactional work
- CoE = expertise, standards, and capability building for complex work
- Best practice: Use BOTH — shared services for execution, CoE for strategy
