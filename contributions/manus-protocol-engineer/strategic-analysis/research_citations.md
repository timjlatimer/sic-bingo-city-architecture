# Research Citations for White Paper

## The Cost of Being Poor / Poverty Premium
- Brookings (Fuhrer, July 2024): Prices of necessities have risen 36% faster than other goods/services over 60 years. More than 1/3 of families overall, and over 1/2 of families of color, garner insufficient resources for basic necessities. 20th percentile Black/Hispanic family income: ~$30,000 vs white 20th percentile much higher. Standard inflation measures overstate income growth for poor families by 18-25%.
- UK Joseph Rowntree Foundation (2024): Poverty premium costs £2.8 billion/year in UK alone.
- Bristol University: Average poverty premium £490/household/year in UK.
- DeFi Education Fund (Nov 2025): "Being poor in America is expensive. Low-income households pay hundreds to thousands of dollars more per year for the same goods and services."

## Unbanked/Underbanked
- FDIC 2023 Survey (Nov 2024): 4.2% unbanked (5.6 million households). 14.2% underbanked (19 million households). Combined: 18%+ of US households don't fully participate in banking.
- Cleveland Fed (2026): More than 18% of US households don't participate fully in banking system.

## AI Job Displacement
- Goldman Sachs Research (Aug 2025): AI could displace 6-7% of US workforce (baseline; range 3-14%). Generative AI will raise labor productivity by ~15% when fully adopted. Unemployment to rise by 0.5 percentage points during transition. Highest risk: computer programmers, accountants, legal/admin assistants, customer service reps, telemarketers, credit analysts. If current AI use cases expanded across economy, 2.5% of US employment at immediate risk. URL: https://www.goldmansachs.com/insights/articles/how-will-ai-affect-the-global-workforce
- McKinsey: 400 million workers globally could be displaced by automation by 2030.
- WEF Future of Jobs Report: 85 million jobs displaced by 2025, but 97 million new roles created.
- Andrew Yang (Diamandis podcast, March 2026): 20-50% of 70 million US white-collar workers could be displaced in next 1-2 years.
- DesignRush (Feb 2026): 200+ AI job displacement statistics compiled.
- AEI (Feb 2026): 122 guaranteed basic income pilots across 33 US states between 2017-2025.

## UBI Pilot Results
- AEI/Corinth & Mayhew (2026): 122 GBI pilots across 33 states and DC between 2017-2025.
- German Basic Income Study (2025): Three-year study busts "social hammock" myth.
- OpenResearch/Sam Altman Texas-Illinois study (2024): $1,000/month for 3 years. Participants reported improved well-being, reduced food insecurity.
- Rizvi et al. (2024): Moderate evidence UBI reduces food insecurity, improves mental well-being.
