## Abstract

The world stands at a precipice. The Fourth Industrial Revolution, powered by artificial intelligence, is not a distant forecast but a present-day reality, set to displace up to half of the white-collar workforce within the next two years [1]. This disruption is occurring within a global system crippled by the inertia of what we term the "Three Gangster Worlds": a **gangster capitalist** system that privatizes gains and socializes losses, a **gangster bureaucratic** state that creates friction instead of solutions, and a **gangster political** class that prioritizes power over progress. These Industrial Revolution-era relics are structurally incapable of addressing the challenges of the AI age, leaving millions of citizens, our "Ruby Reds," adrift in a world where it has become prohibitively expensive to be poor [2].

This paper argues that the prevailing solutions—Universal Basic Income (UBI) and Universal Basic Services (UBS)—are necessary but insufficient. They address the *what* (income, services) but not the *why* (purpose, meaning, agency). We propose a third way: **Universal Basic Purpose (UBP)**, a framework that empowers individuals to find meaning and make a difference in their communities. We introduce a proven, universal, and scalable model for achieving this: the **SIC HB1000 Social Impact Capitalism** framework. This model, built on a "do good, get back" philosophy and powered by a novel Human-AI marriage—the "Master-Jeeves" team—offers a blueprint for a more equitable, purpose-driven, and human-centric future. This is not a theoretical exercise; it is a report from the front lines of a quiet revolution that is already working.

## Chapter 1: The End of the Old World — Confronting the Three Gangsters

For over two centuries, the operating system of society has been built on the chassis of the Industrial Revolution. This has given rise to three dominant, interlocking systems that now act as the primary impediments to human progress in the 21st century.

### 1.1 The Gangster Capitalist World

The modern economic landscape is dominated by a form of shareholder-primacy capitalism that has become disconnected from the well-being of society. As described by Klaus Schwab of the World Economic Forum, the focus on short-term profits has led to a system that often ignores its impact on employees, customers, and the environment [3]. This is the gangster capitalist world, where the rules are written by and for a select few. The result is a widening chasm of inequality and the now-quantifiable "poverty premium," where low-income households pay significantly more for essential goods and services—from credit to groceries—costing the average low-income family an extra £490 per year in the UK and thousands of dollars in the US [4] [5].

### 1.2 The Gangster Bureaucratic World

Government and institutional bureaucracies, designed for a slower, more predictable era, have become sclerotic and unresponsive. As Andrew Yang notes, government is on a "multi-decade tape delay," utterly unprepared for the velocity of AI-driven change [1]. These systems, staffed by good people with good intentions, produce bad outcomes because they are not built for the current reality. They create mazes of paperwork, endless waiting periods, and dehumanizing processes that prevent citizens from accessing the very services designed to help them. This is the gangster bureaucratic world, where friction is the business model and citizens are treated as case numbers.

### 1.3 The Gangster Political World

Finally, the political sphere has devolved into a zero-sum game of power retention, where gridlock is a strategy and progress is a threat to the status quo. This gangster political world thrives on division and distraction, utterly failing to address the fundamental economic and social shifts underway. The result is a catastrophic failure to prepare society for the coming wave of AI-driven job displacement, which Goldman Sachs predicts could impact up to 300 million full-time jobs globally [6].

> "It is one of these three gangster behaviours that is stopping everything. It’s not people; it’s good people with good intentions but bad outcomes because the system isn’t built for them." 
> — Tim J. Latimer, Founder, SIC HB1000

These three gangster worlds form an iron triangle of stagnation, leaving our Ruby Red—the 35-year-old mom trying to make ends meet—trapped, disempowered, and paying a premium for the privilege of being poor. The social contract is broken. The old world is failing. A new model is not just an option; it is an urgent necessity.

## Chapter 2: The Limits of the Current Answer — Why UBI and UBS Are Not Enough

The accelerating crisis of the Three Gangsters has, thankfully, given rise to a global conversation around solutions. The two most prominent are Universal Basic Income (UBI) and Universal Basic Services (UBS). While both are vital components of a future social safety net, we argue they are incomplete. They address the symptoms of economic displacement but fail to address the deeper human need for purpose, agency, and community.

### 2.1 The Promise and Peril of Universal Basic Income (UBI)

UBI, the concept of providing a regular, unconditional cash payment to all citizens, has moved from the fringe to the mainstream. With over 122 pilot programs in the United States alone between 2017 and 2025, the evidence base is growing [7]. Studies have shown that UBI can reduce poverty, improve mental and physical health, and increase financial stability without significant negative impacts on employment [8]. It is a powerful tool for putting a floor under people, giving Ruby Red the breathing room to fix a flat tire without forgoing groceries.

However, UBI alone is a painkiller, not a cure. It provides sustenance but not substance. As Aaron Hurst, who coined the term "Purpose Economy," argues, the next great economic era will be driven not by the consumption of goods and services, but by the pursuit of meaning [9]. A society where a large portion of the population is sustained by UBI but lacks a sense of purpose is a society ripe for social decay, what some have termed the "social hammock" myth, which, while largely debunked in terms of labor participation, misses the more subtle erosion of identity and self-worth that comes from a lack of meaningful contribution [10].

### 2.2 The Utility of Universal Basic Services (UBS)

UBS proposes to make essential services like healthcare, housing, and education nearly free at the point of use, often through technological deflation and government provision. This model directly attacks the "poverty premium" by decommodifying the necessities of life. It is a powerful and necessary complement to UBI, ensuring that basic needs are met regardless of income fluctuations.

Yet, like UBI, UBS is a passive solution. It positions the citizen as a *consumer* of services, not as a *co-creator* of value. It is a top-down model that, while benevolent, can reinforce the very dependency and lack of agency that the gangster bureaucratic world fosters. It answers the question "How will people survive?" but not "How will people *live*?"

### 2.3 The Missing Element: Universal Basic Purpose (UBP)

We believe the crucial missing piece of the puzzle is **Universal Basic Purpose (UBP)**. UBP is not a program to be administered, but a potential to be unlocked. It is the idea that every individual possesses an innate desire to contribute, to make a difference, and to have a sense of purpose. In the coming human-AI dance, where traditional jobs may become scarce, the new work will be the work of community, of care, of creativity, of solving local problems—the work of purpose.

> **Universal Basic Purpose (UBP):** A societal framework that enables and empowers every individual to identify their unique purpose and provides them with the tools, resources, and opportunities to apply that purpose toward the betterment of their community and themselves.

UBP is the active ingredient. It transforms the citizen from a passive recipient of benefits into an active participant in the co-creation of a better world. It is the answer to the question, "What will I *do* all day?" The answer is: "I will make a difference. I will have a part to play."

This is not a utopian dream. It is the foundation of our model. The rest of this paper will detail the SIC HB1000 framework, a universal and scalable engine for delivering Universal Basic Purpose.

### 3.1 A New Capitalism: Do Good, Get Back

Social Impact Capitalism (SIC) is a fundamental reframing of the purpose of business. It moves beyond the limited view of shareholder primacy and the often-vague goals of stakeholder capitalism into a simple, actionable creed: **Do Good, Get Back.**

This is not charity; it is a self-sustaining economic engine. The model is designed to identify and solve real-world problems at a local level—the problems that plague Ruby Red and her community. By solving these problems, the system creates value. A portion of that value is then reinvested back into the system to solve more problems, creating a virtuous cycle. It is a departure from the extractive model of gangster capitalism, replacing it with a regenerative one.

This philosophy aligns with the global movement toward using business as a force for good, as championed by organizations like B Lab and the 8,000+ certified B Corporations that are remaking capitalism from the inside out [11]. Our model provides a universal framework for any individual or organization to adopt this approach.

The SIC HB1000 model is visualized as a North Star guiding a swarm of butterflies.

poverty premium and prove that it is no longer expensive to be poor.

This model provides a clear, visual framework for translating a high-level purpose into real-world action. It allows an individual to define their personal mission (their North Star) and then launch a portfolio of projects (butterflies) to achieve it, all with the support of their AI Jeeves.

This is the engine of Universal Basic Purpose. It gives every person the potential to become a social entrepreneur, a problem-solver, a force for good in their own community, regardless of their background or resources.
